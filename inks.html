THE INKS

01

Plastisol Ink

The workhorse of the industry

80–90%
often cited for plastisol share (estimate)

What it is

PVC (polyvinyl chloride) particles suspended in a liquid plasticizer. It behaves as a paste at room temperature and only cures when heated to 300–330°F (149–166°C), at which point the particles fuse into a solid film. It never dries in air — only heat triggers curing.

How it prints

Sits on top of the fabric surface rather than penetrating fibers. Creates a slightly raised, tactile layer. Very opaque — can cover any color garment with a white underbase print first.

What you can make

T-shirts (all colors) Hoodies & sweatshirts Sportswear Polyester jerseys Tote bags Caps & hats Nylon jackets Corporate uniforms Promotional merchandise Puff / 3D prints Metallic effect garments Glow-in-the-dark prints Glitter effect apparel High-density prints
FeelTextured / raised
ColorsVibrant, exact Pantone match
Durability50+ wash cycles
FabricCotton, polyester, blends, nylon
CuringHeat (conveyor dryer, 300–330°F)
EcoContains PVC — moderate concern
💡 Best for: Dark garments, vibrant designs, polyester, and any job needing exact brand color matching.
02

Water-Based Ink

Soft hand, breathable, eco-conscious

Soft
feel print

What it is

Pigments or dyes suspended in water as the primary solvent. Water must leave the film for the print to set; many textile water-based systems also need heat (dryer or tunnel) to fully crosslink binders for wash durability — check the manufacturer’s data sheet. Contains no PVC.

How it prints

Penetrates into the fabric fibers and becomes part of the garment structure. You feel the fabric, not the ink. More transparent than plastisol — the shirt color shows through.

What you can make

Soft-hand T-shirts (light colors) Premium fashion tees Yoga & athleisure wear Baby clothing Eco-friendly merch Vintage-style prints Fabric rolls (yard goods) Canvas tote bags Distressed-look apparel Muted-color designs
FeelUltra-soft, barely there
ColorsMuted / natural tones best
DurabilityGood (fades faster with washing)
Fabric100% cotton (best)
CuringAir or forced-air dryer
EcoMore eco-friendly (fewer VOCs)
💡 Best for: Light-colored 100% cotton garments, fashion brands, eco-conscious products, vintage and soft-hand aesthetics.
03

Discharge Ink

Remove, replace, reveal

Vintage
look specialist

What it is

A water-based ink that contains an activator (typically zinc formaldehyde sulfoxylate). When heated, the activator chemically removes the garment's existing dye and replaces it with the new pigment color. You're changing the fabric itself, not coating it.

How it prints

Works by discharging (bleaching out) the dye from the garment, then optionally adding a replacement pigment. The result is a print that feels like the fabric — because it IS the fabric. Only works on reactive-dyed 100% cotton.

What you can make

Vintage-look T-shirts Worn / distressed apparel Premium dark-garment prints Fashion-forward streetwear Aged / retro merch Soft dark-base prints
FeelSoftest possible — fabric itself
ColorsMuted, no exact matching
DurabilityLasts life of garment
Fabric100% cotton ONLY (reactive-dyed)
CuringHigh heat activation + dryer
EcoActivator requires safe disposal
💡 Best for: Dark cotton garments, premium fashion/streetwear brands wanting a vintage or aged look without a heavy ink feel.
04

UV-Curable Ink

Instant cure, any rigid surface

Non-fabric
champion

What it is

A 100% solid ink system (no solvent) that cures instantly when exposed to UV light (ultraviolet lamps). No heat oven needed. Used primarily for non-fabric substrates in industrial and commercial printing.

How it prints

Applied through a screen just like plastisol, but cured by passing under UV lamps — instantly solidifying. Produces very crisp, glossy prints. Does NOT work well on stretch fabrics.

What you can make

Packaging & labels Printed glass bottles Plastic containers Promotional pens & drinkware PCBs (conductive pastes, etc.) Solar / electronics (conductive inks) Ceramic mugs Metal nameplates Signage & banners (rigid) Electronic components
FeelHard, glossy surface
ColorsVery vibrant and consistent
DurabilityExtremely durable
SubstratePlastic, glass, metal, ceramic
CuringUV lamp (instant)
EcoLittle solvent evaporation at cure; formulation-dependent
💡 Best for: Industrial printing on rigid non-fabric products. Packaging industry, bottle manufacturers, electronics printing.
05

Specialty / FX Inks

Effects that sell themselves

Stand
out effects

What it is

A family of additive-based inks — mostly plastisol with special particles mixed in — that create visual and tactile effects beyond flat color printing. Some are water-based variants.

What you can make (by effect)

✨ Metallic / Glitter

Metal flakes or glitter suspended in plastisol base. Low mesh required. Gold, silver, holographic effects. Ideal for fashion tees, event merch, accessories.

🫧 Puff / 3D

Expands when cured to create a raised, foam-like 3D surface. Great for logos, lettering, retro sportswear. Very tactile and visually striking.

🌙 Glow-in-the-Dark (GID)

Phosphorescent pigment ink. Charges in light, glows in dark. Safe for children's clothing. Used on safety gear, novelty apparel, event wear.

🌡️ Heat-Sensitive (Thermochromic)

Changes color with temperature. Used for novelty items, promotional products, educational merchandise. Very eye-catching.

🪞 High-Density

Ultra-thick plastisol layer creating a hard, raised, sharp-edged 3D logo. Popular for premium brand merchandise and athletic wear.

🌈 Fluorescent / Neon

Extremely bright colors that appear to glow under normal or UV light. Perfect for sportswear, safety vests, festival merchandise.

💡 Best for: Premium fashion lines, event merchandise, children's wear, novelty products, brand identity apparel. Specialty inks command a price premium — charge accordingly.
