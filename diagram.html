Step by Step

The 10 Steps of Screen Printing

01

Artwork Preparation

Design is separated by color. Each color becomes a separate film or CTS layer. Vector files are preferred for sharp edges.

02

Screen / Frame Setup

Mesh (polyester or nylon) is stretched over aluminum or wood frames. Mesh count is chosen based on detail level and ink type.

03

Emulsion Coating

Photo-sensitive emulsion is applied to the screen in a darkroom. The screen is dried flat.

04

Screen Exposure

Film positive (or CTS output) is placed on the screen and exposed to UV light. UV hardens emulsion in non-image areas.

05

Screen Washout

Unexposed emulsion (in the design areas) is washed out with water, leaving open mesh holes where ink will pass through.

06

Press Setup & Registration

Screens are mounted on the press. Registration marks ensure all color layers align perfectly on the garment.

07

Printing

Garment is placed on the platen. Ink is loaded onto the screen. Squeegee pulls ink across the mesh, pushing it through open areas onto the garment.

08

Flash Curing (Multi-color)

Between color layers, a flash cure unit partially cures each layer so the next color can be printed on top without smearing.

09

Final Curing

Printed garment passes through a conveyor dryer at 300–330°F. Ink fully cures and bonds permanently to the fabric.

10

Quality Check & Packaging

Each garment is inspected for registration, color accuracy, and cure quality. Passed items are folded, tagged, and packed for delivery.
