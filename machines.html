THE MACHINES

Every type of screen printing press on the market — explained for business owners.

Entry Level

Manual Screen Printing Press

The backbone of small shops and startups. You apply ink, move the squeegee, and rotate the platen by hand. Ideal for custom orders, artistic work, and learning the craft before investing in automation.

Output40–80 pieces/hr
Colors1 to 6 stations
Setup cost₹15,000 – ₹2,00,000
Space neededSmall (table-top to floor)
Best forT-shirts, tote bags, hoodies

1 Color, 1 Station

Perfect for logos, simple text, single-color designs. The most affordable entry point. Good for hobby and side hustle.

₹10K–₹30K

4 Color, 1 Station

Print 4 colors without moving the garment — change screens between colors. Great for small batches with design variety.

₹25K–₹80K

4 Color, 4 Station

The most popular manual setup. 4 arms rotate around a central hub, each with a screen. Faster multi-color production.

₹70K–₹1.5L

6 Color, 6 Station

Maximum manual capacity. Can handle complex multi-color designs. Good for growing shops not ready to go automatic.

₹1.2L–₹2.5L
✓ Advantages
  • Low startup cost
  • Easy to learn
  • Flexible for small/custom runs
  • Minimal maintenance
  • No electricity needed (basic models)
✗ Limitations
  • Low output volume
  • Color registration can vary
  • Physically tiring for large runs
  • Inconsistent results vs. automatic
Mid-Range

Semi-Automatic Screen Printing Press

The best of both worlds. The machine handles squeegee pressure and stroke — you load and unload garments. Excellent for shops moving from manual to high-volume without full automation costs.

Output200–500 pieces/hr
ColorsUp to 8 stations
Setup cost₹3L – ₹15L
Space neededMedium (dedicated area)
Best forT-shirts, uniforms, sportswear

Pneumatic Semi-Auto

Air-powered squeegee mechanism. Operator controls the print head via foot pedal. Very consistent ink deposit.

₹3L–₹8L

Servo-Driven Semi-Auto

Electric motor drives the squeegee with programmable speed, angle, and pressure. More precise than pneumatic.

₹8L–₹15L
✓ Advantages
  • Consistent print quality
  • Faster than manual
  • Less operator fatigue
  • Good for medium-volume orders
✗ Limitations
  • Still requires operator per print
  • Higher cost than manual
  • Needs power supply
Commercial Grade

Fully Automatic Screen Printing Press

Industrial-scale production. The machine loads, prints, indexes, and ejects garments automatically. Used by large-scale manufacturers, brand fulfillment centers, and high-volume print shops. Top brands include M&R, ROQ, Anatol, and Workhorse.

Output800–1,500+ pieces/hr
ColorsUp to 12+ stations
Setup cost₹20L – ₹1Cr+
Space neededLarge factory floor
Best forBulk apparel, corporate, events

Oval / Carousel Auto

Oval-shaped conveyor belt with platens. Most common automatic configuration. Handles 6–16 color jobs.

₹30L–₹80L

M&R Challenger Series

USA-made industry benchmark. Advanced registration and automation (e.g. laser-assisted pallet alignment on newer models — varies by generation), PLC touchscreen controls.

₹50L–₹1.2Cr

ROQ (Portugal)

European industrial engineering. Known for precision on fine-detail prints and sustainable operations.

₹40L–₹90L

Anatol (USA)

Offers complete setups including flash cure units. Strong after-sales and parts support globally.

₹35L–₹75L
✓ Advantages
  • Extremely high volume
  • Perfect consistency
  • Handles 12+ color designs
  • Reduces labor dependency
  • Strong automation and registration on newer models (check current specs)
✗ Limitations
  • Very high upfront cost
  • Needs large space
  • Requires trained technicians
  • High maintenance cost
Niche / Advanced

Specialty Screen Printing Machines

Beyond flat garments — these machines print on curved, cylindrical, or rigid objects. Also includes digital-hybrid and CTS (Computer-to-Screen) pre-press systems.

ApplicationsBottles, caps, mugs, electronics
Key techRotary, flatbed, UV curing
Setup cost₹5L – ₹50L+

Cylindrical / Rotary Press

Prints on round objects like bottles, mugs, pens, cups. The object rotates while the screen stays fixed.

Bottles, drinkware

Flatbed Press

Prints on flat rigid substrates: boards, panels, glass. Common in electronics and industrial printing.

Electronics, signage

UV Screen Printer

Uses UV-curable inks cured by UV lamps inline. No heat dryer needed. Used in packaging, labels, non-fabric items.

Packaging, labels

CTS (Computer to Screen)

Pre-press system. Eliminates film — images the stencil directly onto the coated screen (inkjet, DLP, or similar). Faster workflow and fine detail versus many film workflows.

Pre-press essential
